UX/UI Design

Neuron App

Neuron is an existing brand in Singapore that offers different travel services for tourists and citizens. One of them is the bike-sharing system. We offered the design concept that highlights the brand's vision and engages new users.

  • Design Research
  • Wireframing
  • Prototyping
  • UX/UI Design
  • UI Animation

Challenge

Neuron mobile app has complex gamification and sharing functions. We were responsible for making the modern and delightful experience for the users. At the same time, we had the task to translate this complexity to the intuitive and easy-to-use design.

Project Approach

During the project, we asked ourselves the question: how to make the interface for sharing system convenient and useful, fun and playful at the same time? Since the app is oriented towards the young people who is opened to new technologies and active life, we paid a big attention to individual design elements that, in combination with others, will create a style that engages the users and give them new opportunities for activity and communication.

Personas

One of the significant technics we used in design research is the creating personas. To make decisions and prioritizes according to product’s vision and user’s needs personas play an important role. The personas were created based on our assumptions and competitor’s analysis to inform our design decisions. They include 2 types of users which can be categorized into travelers and Singapore citizens.

Competitor Audit

The audit provides the comparison based on analyzing the competitor's functionality and user experience. It helped us identify the Neuron app advantages.

  • The competitor doesn't provide scanning QR code.
  • Competitors don’t provide scooters for the users.
  • Competitors don't have the "Book" device feature.
  • Competitors don’t have a gamification approach.

User Flow

One of the key stages of the design process is the information architecture. Therefore our the next step was to create the user flow which defines the user journey in the app.

Wireframing

Starting from drawing our first sketches with pencil and paper, we've created the wireframes and then translated them to the high-fidelity graphics. During the wireframing stage, we have experimented with different design solutions to select the ones that most effectively would help the user to solve their tasks.

Solutions

  • The interactive map is the first upon opening the app. This allows the user quickly book a device.
  • The "book" feature implemented so that the user can Cancel their booking after confirmation.
  • The interactive map provides the user information about each docking point and free devices for booking. It allows the user easily book device in any place of the city.
  • The dashboard UI is created so that the user can easily track the data about the ride and be confident on any device.

Visual Design

Neuron already has the "Cornflower Blue" as the primary color so to diversify the palette we have picked up the additional colors. Since the main audience is young people who are open to new technologies and social communication, we have chosen colors associated with the game and activity.
